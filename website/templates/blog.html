SDG and sustainable consumption and production

Sustainable Development Goals

The United Nations announced the “17 Sustainable Development Goals” (SDGs) in 2015, provides a blueprint with the ambition to tackle the world’s problems for our planet, people, and prosperity. The SDG’s calls for action by all countries and stakeholders in partnership to implement the plan .

The implementation of the SDG plan is the only way forward to approach the global sustainability challenge of assuring human well‐being, economic prosperity, and environmental protection. For this, all SDGs need to act as a system of interactive mechanism that together move the global system into a safe and just operating space. it’s the collective responsibility of the SDG bodies to see and use the SDG goals as a tool to combat world problems rather than merely seeing it as a structure.

Goal 12: Sustainable consumption and production

SDG12, acknowledges the fact that continuous development and economic growth are determined, depending on how we produce and consume goods. With the continuous projection of food insecurities and food waste across the globe, it’s obvious that the change needed to reverse this trend must be set in motion right away.

By 2030, SDG goal 12 aims at reducing waste generation substantially through prevention, reduction, recycling, and reuse. Standards developed by the international organization (ISO) help businesses integrate environmental aspects into the design and development of products and services and adopt more sustainable practices.
